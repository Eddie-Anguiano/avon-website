Must Haves for Anti-aging Skin Care

With all the thousands of skin care products available, which are the most essential? There are three products that are an absolute necessity for every adult. All three of these products have been around for decades and are the basis for many different formulas. Since they have been around for a couple of decades, there is plenty of evidence that these products are effective. Once you have these three products, depending on your skin challenges, other products may be added as needed. The three products are Vitamin C, Retinol and a good sunscreen.

Vitamin C

Vitamin C in its pure form is Ascorbic Acid. It acts as an antioxidant to reduce damage caused by free-radicals. Free radicals are unstable molecules that cause inflammation. A simple way to think about free radicals is to recall the retro classic video game Pac Man. Free radicals are like Pac Man and the dots that he devours are the molecules of the DNA in your skin cells. Skin cells that have been damaged by free radicals are prone to premature wrinkles, uneven skin tone, premature cell death, dull skin and discolored skin spots. Vitamin C also stimulates the growth of collagen. Collagen is a protein that gives your skin strength and elasticity; that ability to bounce back. As we age, our cells produce less collagen and inflammation, caused by free radicals, can break down the collagen that we have. Collagen rich skin is thicker, more elastic, and less likely to wrinkle.

Retinol

Retinol is a form of vitamin A which boosts the amount of collagen and plumps up the skin. This reduces the look and depth of fine lines and wrinkles. It is the # 1 anti-aging ingredient recommended by dermatologists. Retinol also increases the amount of blood vessels to the underlying structure of the skin and improves its color. As retinol increases skin cell turnover and stimulates collagen synthesis, it also improves the texture and tone of the skin. Vitamin derivatives such as retinol, cause the top layers of the skin to continuously exfoliate, or shed top dead skin layers. This action helps to unclog pores and to keep blackheads and whiteheads away.
